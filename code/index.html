WEBCRAFT

IITM Paradox Technicals presents, Webcraft, a thrilling event for students to exhibit their technical prowess and design skills by constructing a responsive website and wireframe.

Register Now

Description

The event is a Front-end Web Development Hackathon aimed at students with a passion for design and creativity. The first round requires participants to design a wireframe (UI/UX testing) within the allotted time, followed by the development round where they are given a theme and domain to code with their respective wireframe. The judges will evaluate the projects based on predetermined criteria, and the winners will receive prizes for the most visually stunning, innovative, and technically advanced websites. The top participants will get exciting prizes and opportunities. The event consists of three phases: the wireframing round, the front-end development round, and the judging and evaluation round

Wireframing Round:

During this round, participants will be challenged to create a wireframe on-the-spot from the provided domains and themes. Using our Design Partner platform, MockFlow, participants will design their wireframes and submit the image and link to their work via a Google form provided at the event. Apart from the domains and themes which we decided, we will ask the companies to provide us with the domain and theme to code as well.

Frontend Development Round

After successfully completing the first round, We will also be hosting a fun segment where we will ask the participant to take out the chit from the bowl. Every chit has a number corresponding to the particular wireframe, which we will assign to each wireframe. We will ask the companies to provide us with the domain and theme to code. Participants are set free to code on any domain and theme they are interested in, around the Wireframe they are allotted ( Wireframes can be Round 1 frames or Company allotted designs)

Judging and Evaluation Round

Once we got the submission of the applications. There will be a panel of 4-5 judges to evaluate the projects based on the predetermined criteria.

Judging rounds will be held in the booth system where participants have to showcase their application to each judge present in the booth

FAQ's

Find the answers for the most frequently asked questions below

1. Who can participate in this hackathon?
This hackathon is open for all BS degree students with a passion for design and front-end web development.
2. How to pay the registration fee?
Participants have to register from the official paradox Website and then they will have to pay the registration fee from the student dashboard or Paradox's official website. The link for the payment of the fees in the student dashboard will be sent within 3-4 days of registration on the website. Note - No other mode of payment or portal will be there. Beware of scammers or fraudulent websites asking for any sort of payment other than the official student dashboard or IITM Paradox official website. In case of any queries, drop a mail to the paradox team ID -
3. What are the phases of this hackathon?
This hackathon consists of three phases - Wireframing Round, Frontend Development Round, and Judging and Evaluation Round.
4. How will the wireframe be allotted to the participants?
The participants can choose any theme for their website wireframe design according to the domains provided. Wireframes will be allotted randomly in the first round, and participants will have to develop their front-end application/website around the allotted wireframe in the second round.
5. Can we use any front-end framework or library for the hackathon?
Yes, participants can use any frontend framework or library they prefer for the hackathon, from the list of allowed frameworks - ReactJS, VueJS, HTML, CSS, and Javascript
6. How long do we have to complete each round?
The first round will be of 3 hours, the second round will be of 5 hours, and the judging and evaluation round will last for 3-4 hours.
7. What are the evaluation criteria for this hackathon?
he websites developed in the hackathon will be evaluated based on coding standards, code practices, clean coding, and beautiful execution of them bringing the wireframe design to life.
8. What are the prizes for this hackathon?
The most visually stunning, innovative, and technically advanced websites will be awarded prizes. The top participants will stand opportunities to get internships as a Frontend developers and UI/UX Designers from the pool of companies that will be there for hiring from our Hackathon.
9. Is it necessary to host our website on a hosting platform?
Yes, it is mandatory to host your website on any hosting platform like Vercel, Firebase, Github Pages, Netlify, etc.
10. Where can we submit our work for each round?
A Google form will be shared at the venue for submitting your work for each round.
